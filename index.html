About

At an early age, I loved playing with technology. My first experience in tech was my father would bring home discarded computer parts from his work and together we'd assemble a Frankenstein-ish computer monster. Every few months or so we'd find more RAM to install, or an extra 8GB hard drive and upgrade along the way. This hardware experience coupled with the challenges presented by software at the time (Win98), helped pique my interest in finding solutions to the problems inherent where hardware and software meet.

Since then my passion for tech has immersed me in a "Jack of all trades" career path. Over the last decade I've worked for a tech-focused podcast network in Petaluma known as This Week in Tech (TWiT), where I was technical director for "The New ScreenSavers" along with producing & editing multiple tech oriented shows, but also hosted a "maker" show called Know How where we built everything from quadcopters, setting up a home router, GameBoy modding, creating your own raspberry pi arcade system, and more. I also traveled for trade events such as BlackHat, DefCon, CES, and IFA Berlin.

Utilizing my skills in media and tech I moved into conducting large-scale event operations for Facebook & now currently Google. Being immersed in "Big Tech" companies has sparked my interest in deep-diving how impactful coding can be.

I'm currently enrolled in the Thinkful Full Stack Bootcamp with the desire to get hired as a developer.

Pomodoro Timer

GitHub Repository

This was my first project utilizing React. The Pomodoro technique is a time-management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. The technique uses a timer to break down work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. Each interval is known as a Pomodoro, from the Italian word for tomato, after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer that Cirillo used as a university student.
